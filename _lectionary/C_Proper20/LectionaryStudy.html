Amos 8:4-7 and Psalm 113
”...buying the poor for silver and the needy for a pair of sandals, and selling the sweepings of the wheat."

Janet Snowhill commented: I think these words could be spoken to us, who profit on the backs of the Third World in $100 sneakers that some woman was paid $.50 to sew. We wonder what would make anyone hate us enough to fly airlines into our buildings and Amos knows. He knows about the pervasive anger and frustration and helplessness and hopelessness that is suffered in our world so that we in the West can live as we do. Though I do not believe that God is on the side of the terrorists, neither can I believe that he is on the side of the forces that created them. In truth, I cannot see God taking sides at all, but only weeping for all concerned and urging us to find better ways of living together. Which brings me right back to the Psalm and Jeremiah: No, God is *not* with us, and thank goodness! If God were with us it would mean that God is *not* with the needy, the orphaned and widowed, the downtrodden of the world. Frankly, I think they need God far more immediately than we do.
