This is the opening Sunday of our new schedule. Review the reasons and principals of intergenerational study: Children and adults alike are members of the Church. A common pattern is for adults to worship and children to study. But children and adults alike have a duty to attend Divine Liturgy, and to study the Scripture. We approach this study as a way of preparing for worship, so that both adults and children will be able to participate better in the Liturgy.

Jeremiah 4:11-12, 22-28and Psalm 14
I looked, and lo, the fruitful land was a desert, and all its cities were laid in ruins before the LORD, before his fierce anger.

You can find the book of Jeremiah by opening your Bible right in the middle. If you find Isaiah, page forward a bit – Jeremiah comes right after Isaiah. Taking turns reading by section:

Read verses 4.11-12 and 4.22. Remember last week's lesson from Deuteronomy: what was the choice that the Israelites were given? If God's children are foolish, what foolish choices are they making about God's Law (the Torah)? What affect will that have upon the Land?

Read Verses 23-28.

Exodus 32:7-14 and Psalm 51:1-10
Create in me a clean heart, O God, and put a new and right spirit within me.

Exodus is the second book in the Bible. What's it about? -- the Israelites journey through the desert, their receiving the law. What is the Law? There are actually 612 laws, but the Ten Commandments are best-known, and often considered a summary of the law. Rehearse the ten commandments.

Read verses 32.7 to 32.10. How does this relate to the second commandment?

Read verses 32.11 to 32.14. What do we learn about God. Parents or children who have experienced “positive parenting” will be able to identify with God's desire to avoid punishing.

1 Timothy 1:12-17
The saying is sure and worthy of full acceptance, that Christ Jesus came into the world to save sinners

Read Verses 1.12 to 1.14. How is this like the Exodus reading/How does it differ from the Jeremiah reading and what makes God respond differently this time?

Read verses 15 to 17.

Luke 15:1-10
Just so, I tell you, there is joy in the presence of the angels of God over one sinner who repents.

Read verses 15:1-3. What's wrong with eating with tax-collectors and sinners. Who were the Pharisees? Who holds the equivalent position of respect in our culture?

Read verses 4-10. Sing “I just wanna be a sheep”.

Pew-work Hand-outs

“Pew-work” is like Home-work, except that it is done in the pews, instead of being done at home. Because it is focussed on the readings (as the sermon, presumably, also is) it can be done during the sermon to help the listener concentrate. Or, it can be done while waiting for everyone else to finish their communion. It isn't done during prayers, or hymns, or the readings, because

  • During Prayers, we pray

  • During Hymns, we sing

During the Readings, we listen
