Todays reading is the beginning of so-called “Second Isaiah”, written in Babylon near the end of the Exile. Second Isaiah is a collection of promises and encouragement to a people in Exile; a people to whom God and God's promises have begun to seem distant and ephemeral. In what ways do you identify with the original audience of these Scriptures?
