One of our family's Lenten abstinences, is to forego meat on Wednesdays and Fridays. We don't eat meat on Friday's anyway, but now I have to come up with twice as many meatless meal ideas.

If any of you who are abstaining on all or some of the days of Lent, are noticing the same thing I am, then Macaroni and Cheese, and onion omelettes, are getting really boring already. (Those are my stock Friday-abstinence meals, since we do meatless Fridays all year round as the BCP suggests.)

Pamela's Macaroni and Cheese:

  • Combine 2L water and 30ml salt in a large pot and heat on High, to bring it to a roiling boil.

  • Melt 15ml of butter in a small pot (or use 15ml canola oil). Stir in 15ml of flour, making a paste. Add 3ml salt and pepper (and paprika or mustard) to taste -- about 1 ml each. Stir in seasonings. Gradually stir in 250 ml of milk.

  • Grate 150g of cheddar cheese and stir into the sauce. Heat on medium low heat, stirring occasionally, until sauce begins to simmer.

  • With any luck your water should be boiling now. Add 500ml of elbow macaroni to the boiling water; cook until tender (about ten minutes; might be shorter at sea level), stirring occasionally to break up any clumps and keep the macaroni from boiling over. You might have to turn the heat down slightly.

  • Get out a flat wide casserole dish, about 20cm x 30cm. Slice 8 slices of cheddar cheese about 8cm square.

  • Once the macaroni is tender, drain it and pour it into the dish. Pour the sauce evenly over it and stir to distribute the sauce evenly. Arrange the cheese slices on top, and brown under a broiler element until the cheese browns slightly.

Now, that is all very nice, and you are all welcome to try it. It has the advantage that even 75% of my picky-eater meat-and-potatoes non-pietist household will eat it (including my dear husband, who takes a depressingly evangelical view of such things as Lenten abstinence but is willing to indulge me as long as I don't make him eat tofu or nutloaf.

But it won't do if you are fasting from dairy and oil, too, and believe me, it won't do for twice a week for the next six weeks; let alone for six times a week.

So, if anybody wants to offer some additional suggestions for meatless dinners, this is the thread for it!
