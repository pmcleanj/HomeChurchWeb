Bible Passage

Jeremiah 23:1-6

(If you open the Bible right in the middle, you'll probably find yourself in the book of Isaiah. Jeremiah comes right after Isaiah.

Take turns reading aloud verses 1-2, 3-4, and 5-6.

  • Who are “the shepherds who destroy and scatter the sheep”?

  • Who are the shepherds that God will raise up?

  • Who is the king who “shall reign as king and deal wisely, and shall execute justice and righteousness in the land “

Jeremiah 21-23 are prophecies about the last kings of Judah. This is the last part of that prophecy, in which Jeremiah prophecies about an ideal future king. This is one of the first “Messianic” prophecies, that God would himself rule the faithful.
Colossians 1:11-20

Take turns reading aloud verses 11-12, 13-16, 17-20

What do you recognize in common between this reading and the Jeremiah reading?

Paul was a scholar of “The Law and the Prophets”

  • Ask who knows what “the Law” is – the first five books, Genesis, Exodus, Deuteronomy, Leviticus, Numbers; also called in Hebrew the “Torah” and in Greek the “Pentateuch”.

  • Ask who knows what are the “parts” of the Old Testament: Law, Histories, Wisdom Books, Major Prophets, Minor Prophets

As a scholar, Paul was familiar with the Messianic prophecies. In this letter, he describes Christ using phrases and images drawn from those prophecies.

Luke 23:33-43

Take turns reading aloud verses 33-34, 35-38, 39-43

Jeremiah associated the Messiah with the ancient powerful kings of Judah. What kinds of things do we associate with a king?

Jesus was executed as a common criminal. How does God's idea of what it means to be a king differ from human ideas?



Pew-work Hand-outs

“Pew-work” is like Home-work, except that it is done in the pews, instead of being done at home. Because it is focussed on the readings (as the sermon, presumably, also is) it can be done during the sermon to help the listener concentrate. Or, it can be done while waiting for everyone else to finish their communion. It isn't done during prayers, or hymns, or the readings, because

  • During Prayers, we pray

  • During Hymns, we sing

During the Readings, we listen
