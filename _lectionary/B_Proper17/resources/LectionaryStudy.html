1.Under what circumstances does outward appearance matter? When doesn’t it matter? What factors lead people to hide behind appearances? What does one gain by doing that? What does one lose?

2.Consider your own life and relationships. Where are the gaps between how things look on the “outside” and how things really are on the “inside”? How much energy do you put into maintaining those gaps?

3.In light of these three scripture texts, what does it mean for you to live in faithful obedience to God? In what ways do these texts challenge you? In what ways do they encourage you?

4.If Jesus were to address his words directly to you and your congregation, what would he say? What would he criticize? What would he commend?

5.In what ways do you see your faith expressed in your actions? What does it mean for you to be a “doer” of the Word? In what ways do you experience the temptation to be a “hearer only”?

Pew-work Hand-outs

“Pew-work” is like Home-work, except that it is done in the pews, instead of being done at home. Because it is focussed on the readings (as the sermon, presumably, also is) it can be done during the sermon to help the listener concentrate. Or, it can be done while waiting for everyone else to finish their communion. It isn't done during prayers, or hymns, or the readings, because

  • During Prayers, we pray

  • During Hymns, we sing

During the Readings, we listen
