(Adult Student Pew-work ... page 2)

Job 38:1-7, (34-41) and Psalm 104:1-9, 24, 35c
or
Isaiah 53:4-12 and Psalm 91:9-16
Hebrews 5:1-10
Mark 10:35-45

Focus Statement: Whoever wishes to be great among you must be your servant.

  1. In what ways do the demands of “success” in the world compete with fellowship in Christ?

- Successful people associate with successful people ( and tend to shun “unsuccessful” people). The spirit of community is broken. But at the same time, wordly success may be envied – we stereotype people as “suits” and denigrate the morality of people who attain positions of power – eg. “Random acts of management”, “”What do you call a busload of lawyers going over a cliff”, “If you lie down with politicians you'll get up with fleas”

  1. How do your church members struggle to be faithful to these competing demands.

“The Anglican church is the church of Those who have Arrived and of those who have 'just arrived'”. Whether they like sitting next to each other is another matter. The first is a gift of God, the second is a challenge to the Saints.

  1. How is this struggle reflected in your church’s structure? Policies? Practices?

Badly – it is typically the well-to-do and the powerful who gain positions of authority (parish president, vestryman, warden). How much of that is that they bear the skill-sets that made them powerful in the first place? But from their positions of power they must proactively seek to listen to, and represent, God's little ones. Listen to being most important, because the Holy Spirit never entrusts all of God's message to one of us. We hear God's speaking to the church only when every voice in the Church reports what the spirit has laid upon his or her heart.

  1. To what extent does your church associate worldly treasure with the blessings of God?

On this, at least, I think we are fairly sound. Financial blessings are seen as God's gift, but no more markedly so than mere sufficiency, or other kinds of giftedness.

  1. Is there an assumption that favor can be “earned” in the God’s kingdom? Why? Or is there an assumption that hard times or rough moments indicate God’s disfavor? Why?

At times of hardship, the question must always arise as to why God does not reach out god's hand to relieve our burdens. Often there is no answer within our understanding – just as God spake to Job out of the whirlwind. To answer “disfavour” is wrong, just as it was wrong when Job's comfortors answered thusly.

  1. In your church and community, in what ways is service connected to power?
